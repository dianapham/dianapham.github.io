Anxiety

Apr 22nd, 2014

I had a mild anxiety attack earlier. The result of that is that my upper left side is searing in pain. LAMESIES.

It’s something to do with an adrenaline burst, tightly contracting muscles and an abundant acid production. Meh. Alls I know is I felt this feeling for something like three years and I really don’t want to feel it anymore.

I don’t really feel like talking about what happened for three years. It’s not that I’m not willing to, it’s more like I have a ton of work I want to do so I don’t really have time to babble one about that shit. However, I will talk a bit about what my anxiety attack was about earlier.

I have one friend here in Seattle that I’d do anything for: my best friend in Seattle. He’s my roommate now. A lot of people have claimed that we’re dating and whatnot, but they’re just being ignorant. I think of him as a brother. He’s one of the first few people I met when I moved to Seattle. I didn’t meet him right away as if I openly talked to him or anything. I actually hated him for the first year I knew him. He was such an asshole at work. But when I switched to a different department and ended up having to work with him, I realized he was a really good guy. He came off as an asshole because he needed to get his work done. And cuz “someone” needed to put people in their place.

We quickly became friends. We bonded over broski things like UFC, MMA and idiocy at work. I was sick though. I had bad mood swings. I was still trying to figure out my histamine issues. I was still getting hives and migraines. He’d always be there though. He wouldn’t go out of his way to comfort me, but if I ever needed help, he’d be there. That’s all I needed.

Sometimes I’d go weeks not talking to him. Sometimes months. But everytime I reached out, he’d be there. That’s a lot to ask from most people. I don’t know if it’s his apathy that allows him to be like he is or whatever, but I don’t really think about it anymore. We’ve been through so much in our friendship (mostly because of my drama) that we’re just friends now. It’s accepted that we’re friends. Like, forever.

So yeah. The difference with him compared to my other friends is that he’s anti-social and apathetic. I feel like it’d be difficult to hang out with him or talk to him if he wasn’t in the immediate area. So that’s what I was panicking about. He mentioned something about moving to Oregon at the end of the year.

UH. NO.

After a lengthy conversation filled with my dramatics, I believe I secured him staying in Seattle.

Thank gosh.

But yeah, I’m dealing with the reprecussions of my anxiety attack now. It’s annoying, but at least in the grand scheme of things, I still have my friend.

Yay.
